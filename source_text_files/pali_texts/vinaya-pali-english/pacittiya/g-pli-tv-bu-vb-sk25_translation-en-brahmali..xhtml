Khambhakatavagga

The sub-chapter on arms akimbo

 

25. Ukkuṭikasikkhāpada

25. The training rule on squatting on the heels

 

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṁ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme.

At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery.

 

Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū ukkuṭikāya antaraghare gacchanti …pe….

At that time the monks from the group of six were squatting on their heels while walking in inhabited areas. …

 

“Na ukkuṭikāya antaraghare gamissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti.

“‘I will not squat on my heels while walking in inhabited areas,’ this is how you should train.”

 

Na ukkuṭikāya antaraghare gantabbaṁ.

One should not squat on one’s heels while walking in an inhabited area.

 

Yo anādariyaṁ paṭicca ukkuṭikāya antaraghare gacchati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If a monk, out of disrespect, squats on his heels while walking in an inhabited area, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

 

Anāpatti—

There is no offense:

 

asañcicca …pe…

if it is unintentional;

 

ādikammikassāti.

if he is the first offender.

 

Pañcamasikkhāpadaṁ niṭṭhitaṁ.

The fifth training rule is finished.
