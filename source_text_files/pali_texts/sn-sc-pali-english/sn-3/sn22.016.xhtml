22.016. 16. Yaṃdukkhasutta - 16. That Which is Suffering

2. Aniccavagga - 2. Impermanence

 

Sāvatthinidānaṃ.

At Sāvatthī.

 

“Rūpaṃ, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ.

“Mendicants, form is suffering.

 

Yaṃ dukkhaṃ tadanattā;

What’s suffering is not-self.

 

yadanattā taṃ ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na meso attā’ti evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya daṭṭhabbaṃ.

And what’s not-self should be truly seen with right understanding like this: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

 

Vedanā dukkhā …

Feeling is suffering …

 

saññā dukkhā …

Perception is suffering …

 

saṅkhārā dukkhā …

Choices are suffering …

 

viññāṇaṃ dukkhaṃ.

Consciousness is suffering.

 

Yaṃ dukkhaṃ tadanattā;

What’s suffering is not-self.

 

yadanattā taṃ ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na meso attā’ti evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya daṭṭhabbaṃ.

And what’s not-self should be truly seen with right understanding like this: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

 

Evaṃ passaṃ … pe …

Seeing this …

 

nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānātī”ti.

They understand: ‘… there is no return to any state of existence.’”

 

Pañcamaṃ.
