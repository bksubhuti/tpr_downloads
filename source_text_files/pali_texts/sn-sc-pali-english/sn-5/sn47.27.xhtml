47.27. 27. Samattasutta - 27. Completely

3. Sīlaṭṭhitivagga - 3. Ethics and Duration

 

Taṃyeva nidānaṃ.

The same setting.

 

Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavoca:

 

“‘asekho, asekho’ti, āvuso anuruddha, vuccati.

“Reverend, they speak of this person called ‘an adept’.

 

Kittāvatā nu kho, āvuso, asekho hotī”ti?

How is an adept defined?”

 

“Catunnaṃ kho, āvuso, satipaṭṭhānānaṃ samattaṃ bhāvitattā asekho hoti.

“Reverends, an adept is someone who has completely developed the four kinds of mindfulness meditation.

 

Katamesaṃ catunnaṃ?

What four?

 

Idhāvuso, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ;

It’s when a mendicant meditates by observing an aspect of the body—keen, aware, and mindful, rid of desire and aversion for the world.

 

vedanāsu … pe …

They meditate observing an aspect of feelings …

 

citte … pe …

mind …

 

dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ.

principles—keen, aware, and mindful, rid of desire and aversion for the world.

 

Imesaṃ kho, āvuso, catunnaṃ satipaṭṭhānānaṃ samattaṃ bhāvitattā asekho hotī”ti.

An adept is someone who has completely developed the four kinds of mindfulness meditation.”

 

Sattamaṃ.
