51.04. 4. Nibbidāsutta - 4. Disillusionment

1. Cāpālavagga - 1. At the Cāpāla Shrine

 

“Cattārome, bhikkhave, iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattanti.

“Mendicants, these four bases of psychic power, when developed and cultivated, lead solely to disillusionment, dispassion, cessation, peace, insight, awakening, and extinguishment.

 

Katame cattāro?

What four?

 

Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti,

It’s when a mendicant develops the basis of psychic power that has immersion due to enthusiasm …

 

vīriyasamādhi … pe …

energy …

 

cittasamādhi … pe …

mental development …

 

vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti.

inquiry, and active effort.

 

Ime kho, bhikkhave, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattantī”ti.

These four bases of psychic power, when developed and cultivated, lead solely to disillusionment, dispassion, cessation, peace, insight, awakening, and extinguishment.”

 

Catutthaṃ.
