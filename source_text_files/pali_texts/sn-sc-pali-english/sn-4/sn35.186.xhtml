35.186. 186. Ajjhattātītāniccasutta - 186. The Interior Was Impermanent in the Past

17. Saṭṭhipeyyālavagga - 17. Sixty Abbreviated Texts

 

“Cakkhu, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ atītaṃ … pe … jivhā aniccā atītā … pe … mano anicco atīto.

“Mendicants, in the past the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind were impermanent.

 

Evaṃ passaṃ, bhikkhave, sutavā ariyasāvako cakkhusmimpi nibbindati … pe … jivhāyapi nibbindati … pe … manasmimpi nibbindati.

Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind.

 

Nibbindaṃ virajjati; virāgā vimuccati; vimuttasmiṃ vimuttamiti ñāṇaṃ hoti.

Being disillusioned, desire fades away. When desire fades away they’re freed. When they’re freed, they know they’re freed.

 

‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānātī”ti.

They understand: ‘Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is no return to any state of existence.’”
